Diverse Songs for Different Moods

Lord Huron is a band that I've been following for a while now as a large fan of their style of folk and rock music. As such, this is a list of my favorite pieces of music from them over the years.

Setting Sun


Setting Sun by Lord Huron

A slow, guitar-heavy piece about revenge and heartbreak. I find myself coming back to this one often due to the phenomenal chord progression and Ben Schneider's vocal performance.

The Ends of the Earth


The Ends of the Earth by Lord Huron

A lot happier of a song than Setting Sun, The Ends of the Earth is a hopeful yet melancholy anthem about wanderlust and wanting more out of life. The music video is a highlight to me, telling the story of a man trying to reconnect with his lover in the afterlife before they truly move into the great beyond.

Dead Man's Hand


Dead Man's Hand by Lord Huron

While simple and a shorter song than most of their discography, Dead Man's Hand is a showcase of their strengths of a band. Telling a simple but effective story about choosing to live, the use of a bass-heavy backing track carries across the imagery of a scorching desert and the struggle to crawl out of the grave.
